# Backgammon Gym

A native iOS training app (iPhone + iPad) for serious backgammon players. The goal is to train individual skills in a structured way — starting with pip count and cluster counting, with more to come.

The app is **free and ad-free**, and it will stay that way. The full source code is open here. This is a community project, not a commercial one.

## Why I'm building this

I played a lot of tournament backgammon about 30 years ago and travelled internationally for it. Then family and career took over, and I stopped for roughly 25 years. A few years ago I came back to the game — at first only casually online, but by now more seriously again, analysing my matches and playing tournaments.

Along the way I quickly noticed how many skills I was missing to really play well. Tools for focused practice exist, but there's little native to the iPhone and iPad that works offline and feels good to use. Since I'm retired, have the time, and can code, I'm building it myself.

## What the app will do

For the first version:

- **Pip Count** — practising the count, in several stages from guided learning to pure speed training
- **Cluster Counting** — pattern-based counting

Around these, the full training functionality: different practice levels, optional timing (speed matters in real play too), statistics and progress trends.

One feature I particularly care about: below every board, the **GNU and XG position IDs** are shown and can be copied with a single tap. That lets you take any interesting position straight into XG, GNU Backgammon or BGBlitz for deeper analysis. The other way around, you can import your own positions by ID and build personal training collections from them.

Deliberately **not** planned: a play feature. This is about training individual skills, not playing full matches.

## Getting involved

I'm developing this on my own, but I'd welcome advice from experienced players — for example on which methods make the most sense to teach, or which positions work especially well for practice.

If anyone wants to port the app to Android, they're welcome to — I'll support that, as long as it also ends up free in the store.
